📥 How to Import Your Bank CSV
Most banks let you download a CSV (spreadsheet) of your transactions for free. Here's how to do it at the most popular ones:
🏦 Chase
  1. Log in at chase.com
  2. Click your checking or credit card account
  3. Click Download account activity
  4. Choose date range → select CSV → Download
🏦 Bank of America
  1. Log in at bankofamerica.com
  2. Select your account → click Download
  3. Choose date range → select Microsoft Excel format (.csv)
🏦 Wells Fargo
  1. Log in at wellsfargo.com
  2. Click your account → Download Account Activity
  3. Select date range → choose Comma-Delimited File (.csv)
🏦 Citi
  1. Log in at citi.com
  2. Go to your account → Download/Export
  3. Choose date range → CSV format
🏦 Capital One
  1. Log in at capitalone.com
  2. Select account → Download Transactions
  3. Choose dates → CSV
🏦 American Express
  1. Log in at americanexpress.com
  2. Go to Statements & Activity → Download
  3. Select CSV format and date range
🏛️ Navy Federal Credit Union
  1. Log in at navyfederal.org
  2. Select your account → Export Transactions
  3. Choose date range → CSV
🏛️ USAA
  1. Log in at usaa.com
  2. Go to your account → Download Transactions
  3. Choose date range → CSV
🏛️ Most Credit Unions & Community Banks
  1. Log in to your online banking
  2. Go to your account history or statements
  3. Look for Download, Export, or Print
  4. Select CSV, Excel, or Spreadsheet format
  5. Choose your date range and download
💡 Tip: If the columns don't auto-map correctly, you can manually select which column is Date, Description, and Amount using the dropdowns in the import screen.
